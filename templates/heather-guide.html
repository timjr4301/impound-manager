Daily intake — uploading Towbook and BMV files

Do this each morning
Where to go

Click "Daily Intake" in the top menu bar

Daily Intake — two drop zones side by side

You'll see two boxes: one for the Towbook CSV export and one for BMV documents.

Left box — Towbook CSV

Drop the Towbook export here

In Towbook, export your impound report as a CSV file
Drag and drop the file onto the left box, or click "click to browse" to find it
The system reads it and adds any new vehicles automatically
Right box — BMV Documents

Drop your LKA and Title Abstract PDFs here

Download your LKA (Last Known Address) and Title Abstract PDFs from the Ohio BMV portal
The filenames must contain the word "LKA" or "Abstract" or "Title" — otherwise the system won't recognize them
Drop them onto the right box. You can drop multiple files at once.
The AI reads each PDF and cross-references the owner name and address between the LKA and Title Abstract
If the names or addresses don't match between the LKA and Title Abstract, the system will flag it for your attention. Check both documents before sending any letter.
BMV portal link: services.dps.ohio.gov/AbstractAccounts/User/Home

Adding a new impound manually

When needed
How to start

Click "+ New Impound" in the top right

Add New Impound — top of the form showing impound type and vehicle info
Impound type

Choose PPI or Police

  • PPI — Private Property Impound: vehicle towed from private property (apartments, businesses, etc.)
  • Police — Law Enforcement: vehicle brought in by police

Enter the impound date, storage location (e.g. Row C, Spot 14), and police report number if it's a police impound.

Owner information

Fill in the Last Registered Owner section carefully

Owner name and address fields — this is what prints on the certified letter
This address is what prints on the certified letter. Double-check it against your BMV documents before saving.

If there's a lienholder (bank or finance company), fill in that section too. If there's no lienholder, leave it blank — the lienholder letter section won't show up.

Timeline preview

The system calculates your letter due dates automatically

Timeline Preview — shows when Letter 1, Letter 2, and earliest title filing are due

You'll see Letter 1 due date, Letter 2 due date (30 days after Letter 1 is sent), and earliest title filing date (60 days from impound AND 30 days after Letter 2). These calculate automatically based on the impound date you entered.

Click + Add Impound to save.

BMV search queue — working through your list

Daily task
Your queue

The BMV Search Queue shows 100 vehicles at a time

BMV Search Queue — click BMV Done when you find owner info, No Record if nothing came up

Each row shows the vehicle, VIN, plate, impound date, and how many days it's been in storage.

When you find owner info

Click "BMV Done" — enter the owner info you found

BMV Search Complete — type the owner name and address you found, then click Mark BMV Complete

Type the owner name and full address in the notes box (e.g. "Owner: John Smith, 123 Main St, Columbus OH 43219"). Click Mark BMV Complete. This moves the vehicle into Tina's queue once all letters are done.

When nothing comes up

Click "No Record" — this flags it for Tim

No Record Found — this flags the vehicle as urgent and notifies Tim to review
No Record means the BMV has no owner or lienholder on file, or the VIN isn't on record, or it's an out-of-state plate with no info. Always add a note explaining what you found (or didn't find).

A vehicle record — what everything means

Reference
Opening a vehicle

Click any vehicle name to open its full record

Vehicle record — Task Pipeline at top shows where this vehicle is in the process
Task Pipeline

Four tasks — each one unlocks the next

Task 1 — BMV Search

Do your BMV lookup. Mark Done or No Record.

Task 2 — 1st Notice Letter

Send Letter 1 certified mail. Confirm delivery when it arrives.

Task 3 — 2nd Notice Letter

Unlocks 30 days after Letter 1 is sent. Send Letter 2 certified mail.

Task 4 — Ready to File

Unlocks after all 4 checklist items are confirmed. Hands off to Tina.

Certified letters — sending and tracking

Core daily task
Sending a letter

From the vehicle record, click "Print Letter" to get the letter

Printed Letter 1 — Broad & James letterhead, VIA CERTIFIED MAIL, vehicle info auto-filled. Print and mail this.

Print it, put it in the certified mail envelope, take it to the post office. Get a USPS tracking number from the receipt.

Note: The letter says [REGISTERED OWNER NAME] and [OWNER ADDRESS] until you've entered the owner info in the vehicle record. Make sure the owner info is filled in before printing.
UPS tracking also works. Ohio BMV accepts UPS proof of delivery as a substitute for certified mail. The tracking number goes in the same field.
After you mail it

Mark it as Sent and enter the tracking number

Mark Letter as Sent — enter the date sent and the USPS tracking number from your receipt
Go to the vehicle record
In the Certified Letter Timeline, find the letter row
Click the send/mark button and enter the date sent
Enter the tracking number from your USPS or UPS receipt
Click Mark as Sent
After marking Letter 1 as sent, Letter 2 is automatically scheduled for 30 days later. You don't need to do anything else — it will appear in your queue on the right day.
When delivery is confirmed

Confirm delivery after checking UPS/USPS

Check the tracking number on UPS.com or USPS.com. Once it shows delivered, go back to the vehicle record and click Confirm Delivery.

If it shows "Exception" — that means delivery failed. Check UPS/USPS for the specific reason. Then go to the Letters tab and click RTS (Return to Sender) to flag it.

Scanning envelopes when mail comes back

When mail returns
Two ways to scan

Use the webcam or upload an image

Scan Envelope — choose a letter from the dropdown first, then start the camera or upload an image
Click Scan Envelope button (top right of the Letters page, or from a vehicle record)
Optional: select which letter this envelope is for from the dropdown
Click Start Camera (uses the V4K camera on your desk) or Upload Image
Hold the envelope up to the camera so the address and any stamps/markings are visible
The AI reads the envelope and classifies it automatically
What happens after scanning

The system reads the envelope and updates the letter status

Delivered

Logs the delivery date and marks the letter confirmed.

Address Issue

Yellow flag — verify the address and resend.

Service Disrupted

Red flag — immediate resend needed.

Bulk upload

Scan many envelopes at once with Bulk Envelope Upload

Bulk Envelope Upload — select 50+ scanned envelope images at once and the AI processes them all

If you have a stack of returned envelopes, scan them all with the document camera, then go to Heather's Dashboard → Bulk Envelope Upload. Choose all the image files at once and click Process Batch. The AI reads each one and matches it to the right vehicle automatically.

Post office run checklist

Print before leaving
How to print it

Click "Post Office List" → print the page

Post Office Run Checklist — prints every overdue and due-today letter with vehicle info, lot location, and a checkbox

The checklist shows every letter that needs to go out, sorted by how overdue they are. Each row has a checkbox so you can check them off as you go.

File checklist — handing off to Tina

Before Tina can file title
The four required items

All four must be checked before a vehicle moves to Tina

File Checklist — all four boxes must be checked before the vehicle can be handed off to Tina
  • LKA document (BMV 2433) uploaded & confirmed — Last Known Address form from Ohio BMV
  • Title search (BMV 1148) uploaded & confirmed — Title Abstract from Ohio BMV
  • UPS delivery confirmation (proof of delivery) — tracking shows delivered
  • Return receipt — physical envelope filed in cabinet — the green card or signed envelope
All four items are required. The system will not let the vehicle move to Tina's queue until every box is checked. There are no exceptions.
What happens next

Once all four are checked, the vehicle goes to Tina

Tina will see it appear in her "From Heather" queue. She handles the title filing, NADA valuation, and auction routing from there. Your job on this vehicle is done.

Invoice Camera

Bonus feature
What it does

Point a camera at any check or invoice — the AI reads it

Invoice Camera — take a photo or use the V4K webcam to scan a check or invoice

Click Invoice Cam in the top menu. Then click Take Photo or Use Webcam. Hold the document up to the camera. The AI reads the amount, invoice number, and other details and logs the payment automatically.

This is useful when an owner comes in to pay — scan their check or cash receipt and it logs immediately without any manual entry.
